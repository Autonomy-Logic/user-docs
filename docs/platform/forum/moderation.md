# Moderation

The forum has a small set of moderation controls that surface to ordinary users (flagging, special topic states). Most moderation is handled by community moderators and platform admins behind the scenes.

## Topic states

Two badges may appear next to a topic title:

- **📌 Pinned**: sticky topic. Stays near the top of the category for visibility. Used for announcements, FAQs, and pinned threads moderators want everyone to read.
- **🔒 Locked**: no new replies accepted. Used for resolved threads, off-topic threads kept for reference, or threads that have run their course.

You can read pinned and locked topics like any other. You just can't reply on locked ones.

## Flagging a post

Every post (including topic openers) has a **flag icon** at the top-right of the post card. Click it to open the report dialog.

Reason options typically include:

- **Spam**: promotional content unrelated to the platform.
- **Off-topic**: fine content but in the wrong place.
- **Inappropriate**: violates the code of conduct.
- **Personal data**: accidentally posted credentials, customer info, or other private data.
- **Other**: anything else. Add a note.

Add an optional note explaining context. Click **Submit report**.

What happens after you flag:

- The platform notifies the on-call moderators.
- For some flag types (clear spam, multiple users flagging), the post may be auto-hidden pending review.
- A moderator reviews, then either dismisses, edits, hides, or escalates.
- The reporter gets a notification when the report is acted on.

You can't see other people's flags. The total flag count on a post (used by moderators to triage) is not exposed to ordinary users.

## What gets flagged often

- **Promotional posts** with affiliate links, unrelated product pitches.
- **Asking for free unlock keys / cracked software.**
- **Cross-posting the same question in many threads.**
- **Personal attacks.**
- **Leaked credentials in screenshots.** (Common when people share OpenPLC runtime user dialogs without redacting.)

## Moderator actions you might see

Moderators can:

- **Edit** any post (e.g. to redact a leaked password).
- **Hide** a post (it stays in the database but is replaced with "Post hidden by moderator").
- **Delete** posts in extreme cases.
- **Pin / Unpin** topics.
- **Lock / Unlock** topics.
- **Move** a topic to a different category.
- **Ban** users (see below).

When a moderator acts on a post you authored, you'll get a notification with the reason.

## Bans

There are two levels:

- **Temporary ban**: you can read, but cannot post, reply, or DM, for a set period. The platform shows you the duration and reason when you try to post.
- **Permanent ban**: same as temporary, but indefinite.

Bans are issued by admins after a pattern of behavior. Appeals go through the platform's support channel.

## Code of conduct

A short code of conduct lives at the platform's terms-of-use page. The shortest summary:

1. **Be kind.** Help is more useful than mockery.
2. **Stay on topic.** Use the right category.
3. **Don't spam or solicit.** Sales pitches aren't welcome.
4. **Don't share other people's private info.**
5. **Don't ask for or share warez, cracks, or pirated content.**

Violating any of these is grounds for a flag, edit, or ban depending on severity.

## If you're a moderator

Moderator and admin tooling lives on internal pages (e.g. `/admin/forum-moderation`, `/admin/forum-bans`). If you have moderator permissions, those pages will be reachable; otherwise they're not part of the user experience.
