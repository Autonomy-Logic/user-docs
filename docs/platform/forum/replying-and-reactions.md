# Replying and reactions

Once you've found a thread, you have three ways to participate: reply, react, or message the author directly.

## Replying

At the bottom of every thread is a reply editor identical to the **[new topic editor](posting-a-topic)** — markdown, formatting toolbar, attachments. It's collapsed by default; click into it to expand.

Two ways to start a reply:

- **General reply** — scroll to the bottom, type, **Post Reply**. Replies attach to the thread but not to a specific previous post.
- **Quoted reply** — on a specific post, hover and click the reply icon. The editor opens with a blockquote of the original post pre-filled. Use this to make conversation easier to follow in long threads.

### Mentions

Type `@` followed by a username to mention someone. The platform autocompletes from the list of users in the thread, then falls back to general user search.

A mention notifies the mentioned user (in-app notification + email, unless they've disabled @mention emails in **[Settings → Privacy](../../account/settings/privacy)**).

## Reactions

Each post has a vote pair at the bottom-left:

- **▲ (up)** with a count.
- **▼ (down)** with a count.

Click either to vote. Click again to un-vote. Your vote is tallied into the post's score, which affects the topic's "Trending" rank.

There are no other emoji reactions today. The two-vote model is intentional — it keeps the signal simple and gives the platform a clean basis for ranking.

## The author badge

The original poster of a topic shows an **Author** badge next to their name on every post they make *in that thread*. Other roles you'll see:

- **Author** — original poster.
- **Member** — regular signed-in user.
- **Moderator** — community moderator.
- **Admin** — platform staff.
- **Patreon** — Patreon supporter (if the badge is enabled for them).

Badges help skim long threads — you can quickly tell which posts are from the OP and which are help from staff.

## Flagging a post

Each post has a **flag icon** (top right) for reporting. See **[Moderation](moderation)** for what to flag and what happens after you flag.

## Editing your own replies

Same window as topics — there's a short window during which you can edit your own posts in-place. After it closes, you can:

- Add a follow-up reply with the correction.
- Ask a moderator to edit on your behalf.

## Notifications you'll get from a thread

- When someone replies to *your* topic.
- When someone replies to *your* post (any reply directly under it).
- When someone mentions you with `@username`.
- When a post you reacted to gets significantly more votes (optional, on by default).

Tune these in **[Settings → Privacy](../../account/settings/privacy)**.
