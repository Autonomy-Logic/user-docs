# Editing and Deleting Your Own Posts

You can edit or delete anything you wrote on the forum. Topic titles, topic bodies, and replies. This guide covers the three edit surfaces and the rules that apply to each.

---

## Editing a Topic Title

Topic titles are editable only by the person who started the topic.

1. Open your topic.
2. Hover over the title in the header. A **pencil icon** appears to the right of it.
3. Click the pencil. The title turns into an input field with two small buttons:
   - **✓ (checkmark)**: Save the new title.
   - **✕ (X)**: Cancel and restore the previous title.
4. Type your new title. Press **Enter** or click the checkmark to save.

The title updates immediately everywhere it's shown. Activity previews, the category board, and notifications.

---

## Editing a Post (Topic Body or Reply)

Every post you wrote has a **•••** menu in its top-right corner.

1. Click **•••** to open the menu.
2. Click **Edit**.
3. The post card switches to edit mode. The body is replaced with the rich text editor you used to write it.
4. The toolbar, **@mentions**, attachments, and images all work exactly as they do in the composer.
5. Click **Save** to apply changes, or **Cancel** to discard them.

Once saved, a small "· Edited" label appears under the timestamp so readers know the post has been modified. There's no visible revision history. Only the latest version is shown.

> **Tip:** There's no time limit on editing. You can come back days later and fix a typo. That said, editing a post doesn't re-notify repliers. If you make a major change, consider adding a comment as a reply instead so the conversation stays intact.

---

## Deleting a Reply

1. Click **•••** on a reply you wrote.
2. Click **Delete** (red text, trash icon).
3. A confirmation dialog appears:
   > **Delete this reply?**
   > This action can't be undone.
4. Click **Delete Reply** to confirm, or **Cancel** to keep it.

The reply is removed from the thread immediately.

---

## Deleting a Topic (The Whole Thing)

Deleting a **topic** (not just a reply) removes the original post **and every reply under it**. Only the topic's author can delete it.

1. Open your topic.
2. Click the **Delete** button in the header (red, with a trash icon).
3. A confirmation dialog appears:
   > **Delete this topic?**
   > This will permanently delete the topic and all its replies. This action cannot be undone.
4. Click **Delete Topic** to confirm, or **Cancel** to back out.

After deletion, you're taken back to the forum home. The topic disappears from category boards, search, and everyone's feed.

> **Heads up:** Deleting a topic also removes replies written by other people. Use it sparingly. If the topic went off course but had useful answers, consider editing the title and body instead.

---

## The First Post Rule

The **first post** of a topic (the original body you wrote when creating it) has a special relationship with the topic itself:

- **Editing** the first post works exactly like editing any other post (pencil/edit menu works).
- **Deleting** the first post as a standalone is **not possible**: the Delete option is hidden on the first-post `•••` menu. To remove it, delete the entire topic from the header.

---

## What Others See When You Edit or Delete

- **Edits**: The "· Edited" label appears under the timestamp. That's the only indicator; no one gets re-notified.
- **Deletes**: The post or topic simply disappears. Anyone who got an email notification about it still has the email, but the link will lead to a "Topic not found" page.

## FAQ

**Why can't I delete the first post without deleting the whole topic?**
The first post *is* the topic. The entire thread exists because of it. Deleting just the first post would leave orphaned replies making no sense.

**Can I undo a delete?**
No. Both reply deletion and topic deletion are permanent. There's no trash bin to restore from.

**Do people get notified when I edit?**
No. Edits don't trigger notifications.

**Is there a time window for editing?**
No. You can edit your posts any time, including years later.

**Can I see a history of my edits?**
Not from the forum UI. Only the latest version is shown to you and everyone else.
