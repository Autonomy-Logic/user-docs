# Reporting a Post

If you see a post that violates the community guidelines. Spam, harassment, inappropriate content. You can report it to the moderation team.

> Reporting is anonymous to the post author. They are never told who reported them.

---

## Who Can Report

- Any **signed-in** user.
- **Not** yourself. The report button doesn't show up on your own posts. If you regret something you wrote, edit or delete it instead (see [Editing and Deleting Your Own Posts](editing-your-posts)).
- Anonymous visitors can't report. You need an account first.

---

## How to Report

1. Open the topic containing the post.
2. Scroll to the specific post you want to report.
3. Click the **🚩 flag icon** in the action bar at the bottom of that post.
4. The **Report this post** dialog opens.

---

## The Report Dialog

The dialog walks you through a simple form:

### Title

**Report this post** (with a flag icon).

### Description

> Help us understand what's wrong. Select the reason that best describes the issue.

### Reason options

You must pick exactly one of the following:

| Reason | When to use it |
|---|---|
| **Spam** | Unsolicited advertising, repetitive content, off-topic promotion, crypto/airdrop shilling. |
| **Inappropriate content** | Offensive language, explicit material, content that is harmful or disturbing. |
| **Harassment** | Personal attacks, bullying, intimidation directed at another user. |
| **Off-topic** | Doesn't belong in this category (e.g., a general rant in a technical sub-forum). |
| **Other** | Anything that doesn't fit the above. You'll be asked to describe it. |

Click a reason to select it. Selecting swaps to that option.

### Details (conditional)

If you pick **Other**, a text area appears:

- Placeholder: *"Please describe the issue..."*
- **Required**: the submit button stays disabled until you type at least a few characters.
- Give concrete information. "This user is spamming my DMs from this post" is more actionable than "bad vibes".

### Action buttons

- **Cancel**: Closes the dialog without submitting. No record of anything.
- **Submit Report**: Sends the report. Disabled until you've picked a reason (and filled in Details if you picked Other).

---

## What Happens After You Submit

### Immediately

- A toast message appears: **"Report submitted. Our moderators will review it."**
- The dialog closes.
- The post stays visible. Your report doesn't remove it on its own.

### Behind the scenes

- The moderation team is notified.
- They review the post and decide on action: leave it alone, edit, lock, delete, or ban the author depending on severity and whether it's a first offense.
- You are **not** notified of the outcome. This keeps moderation decisions private and avoids retaliation cycles.

### If you've already reported this post

If you click the flag on a post you previously reported, you'll see:

> **You have already reported this post.**

The dialog won't open again for the same post.

---

## When to Report vs. When to Just Move On

Report when:

- The content is clearly against the community guidelines.
- It's likely to harm or mislead other users.
- It's a pattern from the same author across multiple posts.

Don't report for:

- **Disagreement**: If you disagree with someone's technical opinion, reply and discuss. Downvote if you think it's unhelpful. Don't report.
- **Formatting or typos**: Not a violation.
- **Topics you find boring**: Off-topic is a real reason, but only if the topic genuinely doesn't fit the category. Reddit-style "off-topic for my taste" isn't moderation-worthy.

> **Tip:** Before reporting, consider whether a **downvote** or a **reply asking for clarification** would solve the problem faster. Reports are for behavior the community can't self-correct.

---

## Reporting and Privacy

- The post author **does not know** who reported them. The moderation team may see it internally, but it's never displayed to the reported user.
- Reports don't leave a public trail. Other users can't see what you reported, and you can't see what others reported.
- If the post is deleted as a result of your report, it simply disappears. There's no announcement.

|---|---|

| 5 | A toast confirms receipt. The moderation team takes it from there. |
